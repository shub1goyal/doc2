Acme Corporation - Sustainability Report FY 2025

Company: Acme Corporation
Reporting Period: 1 April, 2024 to 31 March, 2025
Report Type: Sustainability Report
Boundary: Standalone Operations of Acme Corporation, which represents 100% of consolidated revenue.

GRI Standards applied.

Section 2: Environmental Performance

GHG Emissions:
- Scope 1 Direct Emissions: 150 MT CO2e (PDF page 5)
  Note: Scope 1 emissions increased by 35% compared to FY 2024 (110 MT CO2e), primarily due to the commissioning of a new natural gas-fired boiler at the Pune manufacturing facility and higher production volumes during Q3 FY25. (PDF page 5)
- Scope 2 Indirect Emissions (Location-Based): 80 MT CO2e (PDF page 6)
- GHG Total Emissions (Scope 1 + Scope 2): 230 MT CO2e (PDF page 6)
- Scope 3 Value Chain Emissions: 300 MT CO2e (PDF page 8)
- GHG Gases Included in Disclosure: CO2, CH4, N2O (PDF page 6)
- Biogenic Emissions: The Company has assessed biogenic CO2 emissions from biomass combustion. This category has been reviewed and assessed as immaterial, as biogenic emissions contributed less than 0.5% of total Scope 1 emissions (approximately 0.7 MT CO2e). Given this negligible contribution relative to overall emissions, this metric has not been separately quantified in the main disclosure. (PDF page 7)

Water Management:
- Water consumption: 50000 KL (PDF page 12)
- Water consumption in water-stressed regions: 10000 KL (PDF page 12)
  Note: Water consumption in water-stressed regions declined by 28% compared to FY 2024 (13,900 KL), as the company relocated a portion of water-intensive operations from the Chennai facility (high water-stress zone) to the Pune facility (low water-stress zone) in Q2 FY25. (PDF page 12)
- Total Water Withdrawal: 52000 KL (PDF page 12)
- Water Withdrawal by Source - Groundwater: 8000 KL (PDF page 12)
- Water Withdrawal by Source - Third-party water: 44000 KL (PDF page 12)
- Total Water Discharge: 2000 KL (PDF page 13)
- Water Discharge - Sent to third-party treatment: 2000 KL (PDF page 13)
- Water Discharge: The company has assessed storm-water discharge volumes. This data has been assessed as immaterial because storm-water discharge occurs exclusively during the monsoon season, is untreated rainwater not generated by the production process, and accounts for less than 1% of total water flows at our facility. No storm-water discharge data is separately disclosed. (PDF page 13)

Waste Management:
- Total waste generated: 25 MT (PDF page 15)
- Plastic waste: 3 MT (PDF page 15)
- E-waste: 1 MT (PDF page 15)
- Non-hazardous waste - Metal scrap: 21 MT (PDF page 15)
- Total waste disposed to landfill: 7 MT (PDF page 15)
- Total waste recycled: 18 MT (PDF page 15)
  Note: Total waste generated increased by 22% versus FY 2024 (20.5 MT), driven by a scale-up of the Raipur manufacturing plant which began full-capacity operations in June 2024, resulting in higher metal scrap and packaging material volumes. (PDF page 15)
- Radioactive waste: Radioactive waste from our operations has been assessed as immaterial. The company does not use radioactive materials in any stage of its manufacturing process, and no radioactive waste has been generated. This category is formally declared as not applicable and immaterial. (PDF page 16)

Air Emissions:
- NOx emissions: 1.2 MT (PDF page 18)
- SOx emissions: 0.8 MT (PDF page 18)
- VOC emissions: The company has assessed Volatile Organic Compound (VOC) emissions from paint spray booths and solvent usage. After internal assessment, VOC emissions were determined to be immaterial, estimated at less than 0.05 MT per year based on solvent purchase records. Formal quantification was not performed as the volume falls below regulatory reporting thresholds. (PDF page 18)

Energy Management:
- Total energy consumption: 120000 GJ (PDF page 20)
- Renewable energy consumption: 30000 GJ (PDF page 20)
- Non-renewable energy consumption: 90000 GJ (PDF page 20)
  Note: Total energy consumption rose by 26% compared to FY 2024 (95,000 GJ), reflecting the full-year contribution of the new Raipur plant, increased production activity across all facilities, and the onboarding of additional diesel backup generators to address grid instability in Q1 FY25. (PDF page 20)
